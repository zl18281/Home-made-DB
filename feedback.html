Perhaps the most common design weakness in your projects, that I haven't necessarily mentioned in your individual feedback, is for your record class to end up too small and the other classes too big, because you haven't made the record class responsible for all one-row operations. That means you are getting data out of records and manipulating it elsewhere. As well as being unbalanced, this is less robust than it could be, because the record class doesn't then give guarantees about all one-row issues.

I am not promising that my final program is bug-free. But I hope you will check it for tips on where to put code, how to make really robust components, how classes can evolve, how to leave user interaction until late on, what kind of tricks you can use, how to refactor (e.g. switching from indexes to keys) or whatever. And you might like to compare your total number of lines to mine, for similar functionality.
